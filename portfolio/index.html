About Me

Guitarist // Producer // Song-Writer

I'm a mixed bag of formally schooled and self-taught as a song-writer, which is an odd combination for most musicians today. But, crossing boundaries and mixing ideas that wouldn't normally don't go together is my favorite thing about art.

Working with me is an endless game of experimentation. Let's take an idea, turn it on it's head, and then try it out 5 different ways. The best version, or at least, the one that feels the most accurate to the moment will reveal itself in the process. The more I've been in music, the more I've been feeling that I don't write songs as much as I learn to discover them.

Experience

My love of atmosphere and cascading dynamic builds has defined a lot of what I produce. Much of my music revolves around creating textures and soundscapes to transport listeners into a sonic story-telling.

Music has always merged out of jam sessions. The Fairlead was the first music project that grew into something bigger than infancy. Opening for acts like Switchfoot, Gungor, and Caspian and playing shows around the Midwest was a great stepping stone for me into the music business and starting a life-long career of music making.

My most current project is one I'm working on with my wife, called Syvers. It's a duo project that has focused mostly on our relationship and processing the ups and downs of being married and the shared experiences that you end up walking through together. Some of my best song-writing to date, especially lyrically, has come through the process of writing with Sarah.

Hobbies

When I'm not making music or behind a sound board, I love playing sports. Pick-up games of basketball and soccer or a good round of golf are all some great ways that I can get out of my headspace and exercise. I've never really been one to spend time in the gym. I don't like lifting weights for the sake of lifting weights, but if you put some sort of ball and rules attached, I'm way more interested.

I also enjoy a good TV show or film. Sarah and I are huge fans of The Office and we quote it constantly. Sometimes, I wish somewhere out there in the real world, there was Michael Scott just doing his day to day work at Dunder-Mifflin. Everybody knows somebody who has some similarities to Michael, but nobody can really pull together all of his unique mannerisms and off-the-wall responses to life. I think that's a big reason why the show is as good as it is.
